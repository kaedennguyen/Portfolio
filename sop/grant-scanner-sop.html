Standard Operating Procedure

Grant Fit Scanner

How a staff member at Harvest Table Food Bank would actually use this tool to decide which grants are worth applying for this month.

1

Open the scanner and check the top-line numbers

The header shows three things at a glance: total opportunities found, how many are High fit, and how many have a deadline inside 30 days. Start here every time — the deadline count tells you if anything is urgent before you read a single grant description.

2

Filter down to High fit first

Click the High filter button. These are the grants worth a real application this cycle. Only move to Medium once High-fit options are handled or exhausted — Medium-fit grants are worth a lighter-effort attempt (like a local retail giving program), not a full proposal.

3

Sort by soonest deadline

Use the sort toggle to put the closest deadline at the top. Anything due in under two weeks needs a same-day go/no-go decision from whoever owns grant writing — don't let a High-fit grant slip past its deadline while still "under review."

4

Read the rationale and the "Watch for" line on each card

Every grant explains, in plain language, why it scored the way it did. The "Watch for" line flags the catch — a matching-funds requirement, a per-location application rule, a narrower service area than it first appears. Read this before committing staff time, not after.

5

Use the search box for a known funder

If someone asks "are we already tracking the Kroger grant," type the funder name into the search box rather than scrolling the full list.

6

Log a decision, don't just close the tab

For every High-fit grant, record one of three outcomes somewhere your team can see it:

  • Applying — assign an owner and the deadline
  • Passing — note why, so it doesn't get re-researched next cycle
  • Needs more info — note what's missing before a decision can be made
Good to know: this demo uses a fixed, sample list of 8 real-shaped grant opportunities for a fictional org, Valley Community Food Bank. In real use, the same tool would be re-run periodically (e.g. monthly) against live search results so the list of opportunities stays current.

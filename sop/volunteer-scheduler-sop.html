Standard Operating Procedure

Volunteer Shift Scheduler

How a shift coordinator at Harvest Table Food Bank would use this tool to fill the week's shifts and know exactly where the gaps are.

1

Add every volunteer once, then keep it updated

For each volunteer, enter their name, check the box if they hold a driver's license (this unlocks delivery-route shifts for them), and add every day/time block they're actually free. Use + Add to attach as many availability blocks as apply — most volunteers have 2–3.

2

List every open shift for the week

For each role that needs covering, set the day, the time block, how many spots need filling, and whether the role requires a driver (check this for delivery routes only). The tool shows an "eligible" count next to each shift as soon as it's entered, so you immediately see which shifts are already thin on qualified people.

3

Watch the eligible count before you panic

A shift showing 1 eligible volunteer isn't necessarily unfillable — it means only one person's availability and skills currently match. Treat anything at 1 or 0 eligible as a recruiting flag: post in the volunteer group chat or make a call before the week gets closer.

4

Run the match

Once volunteers and shifts are both entered, the tool matches people to open spots automatically, respecting availability and the driver requirement. Anything that can't be filled is flagged clearly rather than silently left blank.

5

Work the flagged gaps first

After matching, scan for flagged/unfilled shifts before anything else. For each one, decide:

  • Recruit — reach out to a specific volunteer whose availability is close but not exact
  • Move the shift — shift the time block if that's realistic for the program
  • Cover it yourself or reassign — as a last resort for the week in question
6

Remove people and shifts that no longer apply

Use the × next to a volunteer or shift to remove them once a week is over or a volunteer steps away, so the eligible counts stay accurate for the next scheduling round.

Good to know: this demo starts with a small sample roster (Maria Alvarez, James Chen, Aaliyah Brooks and a handful of open shifts) for a fictional org. In real use, the volunteer list would persist week to week instead of resetting.

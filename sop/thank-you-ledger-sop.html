Standard Operating Procedure

Thank-You Ledger

How a development/donor-relations staffer at Harvest Table Food Bank would use this tool to send thank-you notes that don't read like a form letter.

1

Enter each new gift into the ledger

For every donation received, log the donor's name, the amount given, which program the gift is tagged to, and whether this is their first gift or a repeat gift. These four fields are all the tool needs to write a specific, accurate note.

2

Click the donor to generate their note

Selecting a row on the left produces a full note on the right: a first-gift or returning-donor opening, a middle paragraph naming the specific program and a rough impact estimate (like number of meals), and a closing that scales to the size and history of the gift — a first-time small gift gets an invitation to volunteer; a large or repeat gift gets an invitation to something bigger, like a call from leadership or an annual event.

3

Read it before it goes out — every time

The note is a strong first draft, not a final one. Check the meal-count math and the program name are right for this specific gift, and personalize anything that would clearly benefit from a human detail (a name they mentioned, a note they left with the donation).

4

Send it the same way you always have

This tool drafts the note text only — it doesn't send email or touch a mailing list. Copy the note into whatever channel your org already uses (email, mail merge, or a printed card) and send it there.

5

Flag anything that needs a different response entirely

A first-time gift at a high dollar amount is deliberately treated differently — the note itself says the Executive Director will follow up personally. Treat that line as a task: make sure that call or note from leadership actually happens, not just the automated thank-you.

Good to know: this demo uses eight sample donors for a fictional org, Harvest Table Food Bank. In real use, gifts would be entered as they come in (or pulled from a donation platform) instead of being preloaded.
